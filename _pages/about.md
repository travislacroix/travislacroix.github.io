I am an Assistant Professor in the [Department of Philosophy](https://www.durham.ac.uk/departments/academic/philosophy/ "Durham Philosophy") at [Durham University](https://www.durham.ac.uk/ "Durham"). I am also a Faculty Affiliate at the [SRInstitute for Technology and Society](https://srinstitute.utoronto.ca/ "Toronto SRI") (University of Toronto), an IMH Fellow at the [Institute for Medical Humanities](https://www.durham.ac.uk/research/institutes-and-centres/medical-humanities/ "Durham IMH") (Durham University), and a Fellow of the Higher Education Academy ([FHEA](https://www.advance-he.ac.uk/fellowship/fellowship)). Along with [Carolyn Dicey Jennings](https://faculty.ucmerced.edu/cjennings3/) and [Kino Zhao](https://kinozhao.com/), I am a co-director of the [Academic Philosophy Data & Analysis](https://www.placementdata.com/) (APDA) project.

Prior to joining Durham, I was an Assistant Professor in the [Department of Philosophy](https://www.dal.ca/faculty/arts/philosophy.html "Dal Philo") at [Dalhousie University](https://www.dal.ca/ "Dal") (2021–2024), where I also taught in the Faculty of Computer Science. Before that, I held a Postdoctoral Fellowship (2020–2021) at [Mila - Québec Artificial Intelligence Institute](https://mila.quebec/ "Mila") and the [Department of Computer Science and Operations Research](https://diro.umontreal.ca/accueil/ "DIRO - UdeM") at Université de Montréal. There, I studied ethically aligned artificial intelligence with [Yoshua Bengio](http://www.iro.umontreal.ca/~bengioy/yoshua_en/ "Yoshua Bengio") (Mila, UdeM, CIFAR) and [Dominic Martin](https://sites.google.com/view/dmartin/ai-ethics?authuser=0 "Dominic Martin") (UQàM).

I was also a Postdoctoral Fellow (2020–2021) in the [Department of Philosophy](https://philosophy.utoronto.ca "UofT Philosophy") and the [SRI](https://www.torontosri.ca "Toronto SRI") at the [University of Toronto](https://www.utoronto.ca "UofT"). In this role, I examined issues related to the legal and social regulation of artificial intelligence, as well as foundational ethical and philosophical questions surrounding AI and emerging technologies.

I received my PhD in 2020 from the Department of [Logic and Philosophy of Science](https://www.lps.uci.edu "Logic and Philosophy of Science") at the [University of California, Irvine](https://uci.edu "University of California, Irvine"), under the supervision of Jeffrey A. Barrett. My dissertation, ["Complex Signals: Reflexivity, Hierarchical Structure, and Modular Composition"](https://escholarship.org/uc/item/5328x080 "Complex Signals"), develops an account of how language may have evolved through the modular composition of communicative structures. My dissertation committee included Jeffrey A. Barrett (Chair), Brian Skyrms, Simon M. Huttegger, and Cailin O’Connor.

I previously earned an MA in Social Science (2018) from the [Institute for Mathematical Behavioral Sciences](https://www.imbs.uci.edu/ "Institute for Mathematical Behavioral Sciences") at UC Irvine, an MA in Philosophy (2016) from [Simon Fraser University](https://www.sfu.ca/ "Simon Fraser University"), and a BA (Hons.) in Philosophy and English Literature (2014) from the [University of British Columbia](https://www.ubc.ca/ "University of British Columbia").
